National Rural Livelihoods Mission

The National Rural Livelihoods Mission (NRLM) is a flagship program of the Ministry of Rural Development in India. Launched in 2011, NRLM aims to reduce poverty 
by promoting diversified and gainful self-employment and wage employment opportunities for rural households. NRLM focuses on organizing rural poor into self-help groups (SHGs) and federating them 
into higher-level institutions.

Objectives of NRLM

  1. Poverty Reduction: The primary objective of NRLM is to reduce poverty by promoting sustainable livelihoods for rural households.
  2. Social Mobilization: To organize rural poor into self-help groups (SHGs) and promote community institutions for social and economic development.
  3. Livelihoods Promotion: To enhance livelihood opportunities through skill development, enterprise promotion, and access to credit and markets.
  4. Women's Empowerment: Special emphasis on empowering women through their participation in SHGs and economic activities.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. Rural Poor: The program primarily targets households below the poverty line in rural areas.
  2. Vulnerable Sections: Special focus on vulnerable sections, including women, scheduled castes, scheduled tribes, and other marginalized groups.

Key Components:

  1. Formation of Self-Help Groups (SHGs): NRLM focuses on organizing rural poor into SHGs to build social capital and enable collective action.
  2. Social Mobilization and Institution Building: Creating and strengthening community-based institutions to facilitate collective decision-making and action.
  3. inancial Inclusion: Providing access to credit through SHGs and promoting financial literacy among rural communities.
  4. Livelihoods Promotion: Supporting various livelihood activities such as agriculture, non-farm activities, and micro-enterprises through skill development and training.
  5. Community Investment Fund (CIF): Providing funds to SHGs for community infrastructure and services that benefit the entire community.
  6. Aajeevika Skills: Focusing on skill development and capacity building of rural youth to enhance employability.
  7. Microfinance and Livelihoods Enhancement: Facilitating access to microfinance and promoting financial inclusion for rural households.
  8. Livelihoods in Agriculture and Allied Activities: Supporting farmers with sustainable agricultural practices and promoting diversification into allied activities.
  9. Promotion of Rural Enterprises: Supporting the establishment and growth of rural enterprises through financial assistance, training, and market linkages.
  10. Gender Empowerment: Promoting the active participation of women in economic activities and decision-making processes.
