Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi

  Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-Kisan) is a central government scheme in India that provides income support to small and marginal farmers. The scheme was 
launched in February 2019, and it aims to supplement the financial needs of farmers for the purchase of inputs like seeds, fertilizers, and other resources.

Objectives of PM-Kisan

  1. The primary objective of the PM-Kisan scheme is to provide income support to small and marginal farmers to meet their agricultural expenses and improve their financial well-being.
  2. The scheme aims to ensure a direct transfer of benefits to farmers, reducing intermediaries and leakages.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. Ownership of Land: Small and marginal farmers who own cultivable land are eligible.
    • Small Farmer: Owns less than 2 hectares of cultivable land.
    • Marginal Farmer: Owns up to 1 hectare of cultivable land.
  2. Exclusions: Institutional landholders, individuals who have retired from government service, and those who pay income tax are not eligible.

Key Components:

  1. Financial Assistance: Eligible farmers receive direct income support of Rs. 6,000 per year, provided in three equal installments of Rs. 2,000 each.
  2. Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT): The financial assistance is transferred directly to the Aadhaar-linked bank accounts of the beneficiaries to ensure transparency and efficiency.
  3. Enrollment Process: Farmers can enroll for the PM-Kisan scheme through the official PM-Kisan portal, Common Service Centers (CSCs), or by visiting the nearest agriculture department office.
  4. Identification of Beneficiaries: States and Union Territories are responsible for identifying eligible farmers and uploading their details on the PM-Kisan portal.
  5. Exclusion Criteria: Institutional landholders, farmers who have retired from government service, and those who pay income tax are excluded from the scheme.
  6. Funding: The scheme is funded by the central government.
