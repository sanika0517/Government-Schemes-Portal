Dhanalakshmi Scheme 

 Dhanalakshmi Scheme is a Conditional cash transfer Scheme for the girl child with insurance cover. It was launched on 3 March 2008 by the Ministry of Women and Child Development, Government of India. The scheme aimed at doing away with child marriage by offering parents an attractive insurance cover, and encouraging parents to educate their children as well as covering certain medical expenses for girl babies. The aim of the scheme was to value the life of a girl child and not treat them as a liability.

Objectives of Dhanalakshmi Scheme

  1. Create a mindset amongst people to value the life of girl children and not treat them as liabilities
  2. Encourage girl children to enrol in schools, continue schooling at least up to class 8 and delay marriage till they attain 18 years
  3. Empower women with financial independence and increase their self-esteem
  4. Prevent female infanticide by providing insurance coverage
  5. Remove the stigma attached to a girl’s birth as a bad omen and highlight the girl’s worth as an equal family member
  6. Equip a girl to avail of job opportunities by encouraging her education and look forward to a promising future

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. A girl child born after 8th November 2008
  2. Every girl child, irrespective of her socio-economic status
  3. Every girl child in the family, regardless of the number of girls
  4. The girl child must be covered under the immunisation program
  5. The girl child should be an Indian resident

Key Components:

  1. At the birth registration of a girl child - Rs.5,000
  2. For immunisation spread across different infancy stages - Rs.1,250
  3. At the enrollment and completion of primary schooling - Rs.3,500
  4. At enrollment for secondary schooling up to class 8 - Rs.3,750
  5. When the girl child is 18 years - insurance maturity cover of Rs.1 lakh
