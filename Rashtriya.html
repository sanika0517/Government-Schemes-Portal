Rashtriya Mahila Kisan Yojana

 Rashtriya Mahila Kisan Yojana is a scheme implemented by the Ministry of Rural Development in India. The scheme focuses on empowering women in 
 agriculture by enhancing their participation, productivity, and income.

Objectives Of Rashtriya Mahila Kisan Yojana

  1. Empowerment: The primary objective is to empower women engaged in agricultural activities by providing them with the necessary support, resources, and opportunities.
  2. Productivity: Enhance the productivity of women farmers through the adoption of modern and sustainable agricultural practices.
  3. Income Generation: Increase the income of women in agriculture by promoting diversification into income-generating activities, such as livestock rearing, poultry farming, and agro-processing.
  4. Skill Development: Provide training and capacity-building programs to equip women with the skills and knowledge required for improved agricultural practices.
  5. Access to Resources: Ensure that women farmers have better access to agricultural resources, including credit, technology, seeds, and other inputs.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. Women Farmers: The primary beneficiaries are usually women who are actively engaged in agricultural activities. This may include women who own or operate farmland or participate in various aspects of farming.
  2. Residence: Beneficiaries are typically required to be residents of rural areas.
  3. Membership in Self-Help Groups (SHGs): In some cases, participation in or formation of self-help groups may be a criterion for eligibility.
  4. Socio-Economic Status: The scheme may target women from economically disadvantaged backgrounds.
  5. Land Ownership: Depending on the specific objectives of the scheme, eligibility may be linked to landownership or participation in agricultural activities.
  6. Age Criteria: Some schemes may have age-related eligibility criteria for women to participate.

Key Components:

  1. Capacity Building: Training and skill development programs for women farmers to enhance their knowledge and capabilities in various aspects of agriculture.
  2. Financial Support: Provision of financial assistance or subsidies to support women in agriculture. This may include funds for the purchase of seeds, fertilizers, equipment, etc.
  3. Livelihood Diversification: Encouraging women to diversify their livelihoods by engaging in allied activities like animal husbandry, poultry, and other income-generating ventures.
  4. Community Mobilization: Facilitating the formation of self-help groups and encouraging community participation to create a supportive environment for women in agriculture.
  5. Gender Equity: Promoting gender equity and addressing the specific challenges faced by women in the agricultural sector.
