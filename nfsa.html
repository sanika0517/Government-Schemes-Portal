The NFSA is a landmark legislation aimed at ensuring food security for all citizens of the country. It seeks to provide subsidized food grains to eligible beneficiaries 
through the Targeted Public Distribution System (TPDS).

Objectives of NFSA

  1. Food Security:Ensure food security for the entire population of the country.
  2. Subsidized Food Grains:Provide subsidized food grains to identified beneficiaries through the TPDS.
  3. Nutritional Support:Improve nutritional levels among vulnerable sections of society.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. Priority Households:Priority households are entitled to receive a specific quantity of food grains at subsidized rates. These households are identified based on predefined criteria such as income, occupation, and socio-economic status.
  2. Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) Families:AAY families are considered the most vulnerable and receive a higher quantity of subsidized food grains. This category includes the poorest of the poor.
  3. Exclusion Criteria:Certain exclusion criteria are defined to ensure that only the intended beneficiaries receive the benefits. For example, households with income above a certain threshold or those owning specified assets may be excluded.
  4. Identification Process:States and Union Territories conduct surveys and use various parameters to identify and categorize eligible households. The identification process may involve the use of socio-economic criteria, family income, occupation, and demographic details.
  5. Special Provisions:The NFSA includes special provisions for pregnant women, lactating mothers, and children under the Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) and Mid-Day Meal (MDM) schemes. These groups may have additional eligibility criteria.
  6. Children's Entitlement:Children up to the age of 14 are entitled to nutritious meals under the NFSA, and specific eligibility criteria may apply.

Key Components:

  1. Identification of Beneficiaries:States and Union Territories are responsible for identifying eligible households and individuals entitled to receive subsidized food grains.
  2. Entitlements:Specifies the quantity of food grains to be provided to eligible beneficiaries at subsidized rates. Priority households and Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) families receive a higher quantity of grains.
  3. Targeted Public Distribution System (TPDS):Implements the distribution of food grains through a network of Fair Price Shops (FPS) to ensure efficient delivery.
  4. Subsidized Rates:Food grains are provided at highly subsidized rates. The prices are fixed by the central and state governments.
  5. Special Provisions for Vulnerable Groups:Special provisions for pregnant women, lactating mothers, and children under the Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) and Mid-Day Meal (MDM) schemes.
  6. Legal Entitlement:Recognizes food as a legal entitlement and empowers beneficiaries to receive food grains under the law.
  7. Implementation by States:The implementation and execution of the NFSA largely depend on the states and Union Territories.
