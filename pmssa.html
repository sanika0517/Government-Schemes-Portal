Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyaan 

 
        The Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA) was launched by the Government of India to provide comprehensive and quality antenatal care to pregnant women, 
        especially those in their second and third trimesters.

Objectives of PMSMA

  1. To provide comprehensive antenatal care to all pregnant women.
  2. To reduce the maternal and neonatal mortality and morbidity rates.
  3. To promote safe motherhood by detecting and managing high-risk pregnancies.
  4. To ensure quality maternal health services, including check-ups and diagnostics, for all pregnant women.

Eligibility Criteria:

         All pregnant women, especially those in their second and third trimesters, are eligible to avail the benefits of PMSMA. The program aims to provide services to all pregnant women to 
        ensure their well-being and reduce complications during pregnancy and childbirth.

Key Components:

  1. Antenatal Check-ups: PMSMA focuses on providing pregnant women with regular antenatal check-ups, including consultations with doctors, health assessments, and necessary tests to monitor their health and the health of the fetus.
  2. Free Services on 9th of Every Month: Under the PMSMA, free antenatal services are provided to pregnant women on the 9th of every month. This initiative aims to ensure that pregnant women receive timely and quality care throughout their pregnancy.
  3. Quality Antenatal Care: The program emphasizes providing quality antenatal care to pregnant women by ensuring that they receive the necessary medical consultations, screenings, and interventions based on their individual needs and risk factors.
  4. Identification and Management of High-risk Pregnancies: PMSMA focuses on identifying and managing high-risk pregnancies by conducting screenings, assessments, and referrals to ensure that pregnant women receive appropriate care and interventions to prevent complications.
  5. Awareness and Education: The program aims to raise awareness about the importance of antenatal care, safe motherhood practices, nutrition, and other essential aspects of pregnancy and childbirth among pregnant women, families, and communities.
  6. Public-Private Partnership: PMSMA encourages collaboration between the government and private healthcare providers to ensure that pregnant women receive quality antenatal care services, including consultations, diagnostics, and treatments, based on their needs and preferences.
