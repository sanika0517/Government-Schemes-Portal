Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram 

 
        The Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK) is an initiative launched by the Government of India to provide comprehensive healthcare to children under the age of 18 years. 
        The main aim of the RBSK is early detection and management of 4 D's—Defects at birth, Diseases in children, Deficiencies, and Developmental delays, including disabilities.

Objectives of RBSK

  1. Comprehensive Health Check-up: The RBSK scheme aims to provide a comprehensive health check-up for children, covering a wide range of health conditions.
  2. Free Treatment: Once a child is diagnosed with any of the 30 health conditions covered under the scheme, they are provided with free treatment or referred to higher healthcare facilities for specialized care.
  3. Inclusive Approach: The RBSK scheme adopts an inclusive approach, targeting children from all sections of society, including those from marginalized communities.
  4. Integration with Other Schemes: The RBSK scheme is integrated with other healthcare initiatives to ensure a holistic approach to child health.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. Age Criteria: Children up to the age of 18 years are eligible for screening under the RBSK scheme.
  2. Screening Points: Screening is conducted at various levels
    • At birth in health facilities.
    • In schools.
    • In community settings through Mobile Health Teams.
  3. Target Group: The scheme focuses on children in rural areas, marginalized sections of society, and those with limited access to healthcare services.

Key Components:

  1. Screening: All children up to 18 years of age are screened for 30 health conditions, including birth defects, deficiencies, diseases, and developmental delays.
  2. Early Intervention Services: Once identified, children are referred to the appropriate facility for free treatment or intervention.
  3. and Follow-up: After the screening, children identified with health conditions are provided with the necessary medical treatment and follow-up services.
