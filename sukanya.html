Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana 

 Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) is a savings scheme launched by the Government of India to encourage parents to save for the future education and marriage expenses 
    of their girl child.

Objectives of SSY

  1. Promote Girl Child Education and Marriage:The primary objective is to encourage parents to save for the higher education and marriage expenses of their girl child.
  2. Financial Security:Provide a long-term savings avenue to ensure financial security for the girl child.
  3. Empowerment of Girls: Promote the financial empowerment of girls by encouraging savings in their names.
  4. Tax Benefits:Offer income tax benefits to parents or legal guardians, making it an attractive savings option.
  5. Discourage Female Foeticide:By providing incentives for saving for the girl child, the scheme indirectly contributes to the government's efforts to discourage female foeticide.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. Age of Girl Child:The scheme is applicable for a girl child below the age of 10 years at the time of opening the account.
  2. Citizenship:The girl child should be a resident of India.
  3. umber of Accounts:Parents or legal guardians can open only one account per girl child.

Key Components:

  1. Account Opening:The scheme allows parents or legal guardians to open a savings account in the name of their girl child.
  2. Age Limit:The account can be opened for a girl child below the age of 10 years.
  3. Deposits:Deposits can be made in the account until the completion of 15 years from the date of opening the account.
  4. Interest Rate:The interest rate is fixed by the government and is typically higher than that offered by regular savings accounts. The interest is compounded annually.
  5. Minimum and Maximum Deposit:The minimum deposit amount is nominal, and there is a maximum limit for annual deposits.
