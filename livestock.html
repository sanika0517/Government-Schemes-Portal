National Livestock Mission

 National Livestock Mission (NLM) is a centrally sponsored scheme in India aimed at enhancing livestock productivity and improving the livelihoods of livestock farmers. The 
mission was launched as a part of the National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) to promote holistic growth of the livestock sector.

Objectives of National Livestock Mission

  1. Improve livestock productivity and production systems.
  2. Promote sustainable practices in livestock management.
  3. Enhance the livelihoods of small and marginal farmers.
  4. Improve the availability of quality feed and fodder.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. Category of Farmers:
    • Small and Marginal Farmers: Those who own or cultivate land up to 2 hectares.
    • Landless Laborers: Individuals who may not own land but are involved in livestock-related activities.
  2. Livestock Ownership:Eligibility often depends on ownership or active involvement in livestock rearing activities.
  3. Geographical Considerations:The eligibility may also be influenced by the location of the farmer, as schemes are often designed to target specific regions or states.
  4. Participation in Livestock Development Programs:Farmers willing to participate in livestock development programs, such as breed improvement, veterinary healthcare, and other related activities.
  5. inancial Status:Small and economically disadvantaged farmers are typically prioritized.
  6. Women and Socially Disadvantaged Groups:Special consideration may be given to women engaged in livestock rearing and socially disadvantaged groups.
  7. Adherence to Program Guidelines:Farmers must adhere to the guidelines and requirements outlined for specific sub-schemes or components under the National Livestock Mission.

Key Components:

  1. Livestock Development:
    • Promoting breed improvement programs.
    • Strengthening veterinary healthcare services.
  2. Fodder and Feed Development:
    • Promoting improved fodder production and management.
    • Enhancing feed availability and quality.
  3. Risk Management and Insurance:
    • Providing support for livestock insurance.
    • Implementing measures for risk management.
  4. Institutional Support:
    • Strengthening institutions related to livestock development.
    • Promoting research and development in the livestock sector.
  5. Livestock Marketing Infrastructure: Developing market infrastructure for livestock and livestock products.
