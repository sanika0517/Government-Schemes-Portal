Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram 

 
        The Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK) is an initiative launched by the Government of India to provide free maternal and neonatal healthcare services to pregnant 
        women and infants.

Objectives of JSSK

  1. Free Maternal Services: Ensuring that pregnant women receive free and cashless delivery services, including cesarean sections, in public health institutions.
  2. Free Newborn Care: Providing essential newborn care and treatment for sick newborns at public health facilities without any out-of-pocket expenses for families.
  3. Free Drugs and Consumables: Ensuring availability of free drugs, diagnostics, and consumables required for maternal and newborn care services.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. Pregnant Women: All pregnant women delivering in public health institutions are eligible for free and cashless delivery services under the JSSK scheme.
  2. Infants: The scheme covers all sick newborns requiring treatment and care in public health facilities.
  3. Public Health Facilities: The services provided under JSSK are available at all public health institutions, including primary health centers, community health centers, district hospitals, and other government hospitals.

Key Components:

  1. Free Transport: The scheme provides free transport facilities for pregnant women and sick infants to and from health institutions.
  2. Free Diagnostics: Essential diagnostic services like blood tests, ultrasound, and other necessary investigations are provided free of cost.
  3. ree Drugs and Consumables: Medicines, consumables, and other essential items required for maternal and newborn care are provided without any charges.
