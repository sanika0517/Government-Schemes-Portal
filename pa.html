Poshan Abhiyaan 

 
        Poshan Abhiyaan, launched by the Government of India in 2018, is a flagship program aimed at improving nutritional outcomes for children, pregnant women, and lactating mothers. 
        It emphasizes the importance of adequate nutrition during the first 1,000 days of a child's life, from conception until the age of two.

Objectives of Poshan Abhiyaan

  1. Reduce stunting among children (0-6 years).
  2. Prevent and reduce undernutrition (prevalence of underweight children under 5 years).
  3. Reduce the prevalence of anemia among young children, adolescents, and women.
  4. Reduce low birth weight.
  5. Ensure the convergence of various nutrition-related schemes and programs.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. Pregnant women
  2. Lactating mothers
  3. Under the age of 6 years

Key Components:

  1. ICDS-CAS (Integrated Child Development Services - Common Application Software): A technological platform to monitor the delivery of nutritional services at the grassroots level.
  2. Poshan Abhiyaan emphasizes the convergence of various departments and ministries like Health, Women and Child Development, Rural Development, and others. This ensures holistic development and implementation of nutrition-related interventions.
  3. Dashboard and Management Information System (MIS): It enables real-time monitoring of the program's implementation at all levels.
  4. ICT-RTM (Information and Communication Technology enabled Real-Time Monitoring): This involves leveraging technology for real-time tracking of beneficiary services and nutritional outcomes.
  5. Nutrition Rehabilitation Centers (NRCs): Specialized centers to treat severely malnourished children with medical complications.
