Mahila Kisan Sashaktikaran Pariyojana

 The Mahila Kisan Sashaktikaran Pariyojana (MKSP) is a Government of India initiative aimed at empowering women in agriculture. Launched by the Ministry of Rural Development, MKSP focuses on improving the participation of women in agriculture and enhancing their productivity and income. The scheme aims to address the specific needs and challenges faced by women in rural areas who are engaged in agriculture.

Objectives of Mahila Kisan Sashaktikaran Pariyojana

  1. Empowering Women Farmers: The primary goal is to empower women involved in agriculture by providing them with the necessary knowledge, skills, and resources to enhance their participation in farming activities.
  2. nhancing Productivity: The scheme aims to improve agricultural productivity by promoting sustainable and efficient farming practices among women farmers.
  3. Income Generation: MKSP focuses on increasing the income of women engaged in agriculture by promoting diversified and value-added activities. This may include activities like dairy farming, poultry, handicrafts, and other income-generating ventures.
  4. Capacity Building: The program involves capacity-building initiatives to equip women farmers with the skills and knowledge required for modern and sustainable agricultural practices.
  5. Access to Resources: MKSP strives to ensure that women farmers have better access to agricultural resources, including credit, technology, seeds, and other inputs.
  6. Promoting Gender Equity: The scheme places a strong emphasis on promoting gender equity in the agricultural sector, recognizing the crucial role played by women in farming and ensuring their fair share of benefits.
  7. Community Participation: MKSP encourages the active participation of women in community-based organizations, self-help groups, and other collective efforts that can contribute to the overall development of rural areas.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. Women Farmers: The primary beneficiaries are women actively engaged in agricultural activities. This may include women who own or operate farmland or participate in various aspects of farming.
  2. Residence: Beneficiaries are often required to be residents of rural areas.
  3. Membership in Self-Help Groups (SHGs): In some cases, participation in or formation of self-help groups may be a criterion for eligibility.
  4. Socio-Economic Status: The scheme may target women from economically disadvantaged backgrounds.

Key Components:

  1. Capacity Building: Training and capacity-building initiatives for women farmers to enhance their knowledge and skills in modern and sustainable agricultural practices.
  2. Financial Support: Provision of financial assistance or subsidies to support women in agriculture. This may include funds for the purchase of seeds, fertilizers, equipment, etc.
  3. Income Generation Activities: Promotion of income-generating activities and diversification of sources of income for women, such as dairy farming, poultry, handicrafts, etc.
  4. Access to Resources: Ensuring women have better access to agricultural resources, including credit, technology, seeds, and other inputs.
  5. Community Participation: Encouraging the active participation of women in community-based organizations and self-help groups, fostering a sense of community and mutual support.
  6. Gender Equity: Promoting gender equity in the agricultural sector, recognizing and addressing the specific challenges faced by women in farming.
