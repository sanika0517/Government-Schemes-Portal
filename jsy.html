Janani Suraksha Yojana 

 
        Janani Suraksha Yojana is a centrally sponsored scheme launched by the Government of India with the aim of reducing maternal and infant mortality by promoting institutional deliveries 
        among pregnant women. The scheme provides financial assistance to pregnant women to ensure that they deliver their babies in a healthcare facility.

Objectives of Janani Suraksha Yojana

  1. Reduce Maternal Mortality Rate (MMR):The primary objective of JSY is to reduce maternal mortality by promoting institutional deliveries, ensuring that pregnant women have access to skilled care during childbirth, thereby reducing complications and deaths related to pregnancy and childbirth.
  2. Reduce Infant Mortality Rate (IMR):Another crucial objective of the scheme is to reduce the infant mortality rate by ensuring that newborns receive immediate medical attention, care, and vaccinations in healthcare facilities after birth.
  3. Promote Institutional Deliveries: JSY aims to increase the percentage of institutional deliveries among pregnant women, particularly those from marginalized and economically disadvantaged backgrounds, by providing financial incentives, transportation support, and promoting awareness about the benefits of skilled care during childbirth.
  4. Enhance Access to Quality Healthcare: The scheme aims to improve access to quality healthcare services, including antenatal, intranatal, and postnatal care, for pregnant women and newborns by strengthening healthcare infrastructure, facilities, and human resources in both rural and urban areas.
  5. Empower Women and Families: JSY focuses on empowering women and their families by raising awareness about maternal and child health, promoting gender equality, and providing counseling services to address misconceptions, cultural barriers, and socio-economic factors affecting healthcare-seeking behavior among pregnant women.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. Pregnant Women:Pregnant women, particularly those from marginalized and economically weaker sections, are eligible to avail of the benefits under the JSY scheme.
  2. Age Criteria:There is no specific age limit mentioned; however, the primary focus is on providing financial assistance to pregnant women from vulnerable and economically disadvantaged backgrounds.
  3. Antenatal Check-ups: Women who have undergone at least one antenatal check-up during their pregnancy are eligible for the benefits under the JSY scheme.
  4. Institutional Delivery:To avail of the benefits, pregnant women must opt for institutional delivery in a recognized healthcare facility, including government hospitals, community health centers, and accredited private institutions.

Key Components:

  1. Cash Assistance: JSY provides cash assistance to pregnant women who opt for institutional deliveries, ensuring that they receive skilled care during childbirth.
  2. Incentives: The scheme offers financial incentives to pregnant women, particularly those from marginalized and economically weaker sections, to encourage them to deliver in healthcare institutions.
  3. Transportation Support: JSY includes provisions for providing transportation support to pregnant women residing in remote and inaccessible areas to ensure timely access to healthcare facilities for institutional delivery.
  4. Skilled Care: The scheme emphasizes the importance of skilled care during childbirth and promotes institutional deliveries where pregnant women can receive appropriate medical care, including antenatal, intranatal, and postnatal services.
  5. Awareness and Counseling: JSY also focuses on raising awareness about the benefits of institutional deliveries and providing counseling services to pregnant women and their families to address their concerns and misconceptions related to childbirth.
