Udaan 

 UDAAN is a project launched by the CBSE under the aegis of Ministry of Human Resource Development (MHRD) to address the low enrolment of girl students in prestigious 
engineering institutions and the teaching gap between school education and engineering entrance examinations.

Objectives of Udaan

  1. Skill Development:To enhance the skills of the youth in Jammu and Kashmir and make them more employable in various sectors.
  2. Employment Generation:To generate employment opportunities for the youth by connecting them with potential employers and industries.
  3. Inclusive Development:To promote inclusive development by providing opportunities to the youth, thereby contributing to the overall socio-economic growth of the region.
  4. Reducing Unemployment:To address the issue of unemployment by creating a bridge between skilled individuals and industries in need of such skills.
  5. Integration with National Skill Development Initiatives:Udaan may align with national skill development initiatives to ensure that the youth in Jammu and Kashmir can benefit from broader programs and opportunities.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. Residence:The scheme is typically targeted at the youth of Jammu and Kashmir.
  2. Educational Qualification:Eligibility criteria may vary based on the specific training programs, but participants often include individuals with varying educational backgrounds.
  3. Age Criteria:The scheme may have specific age criteria for participation.
  4. Unemployed Youth: Generally, the focus is on providing opportunities to unemployed youth who are seeking skill development and employment.

Key Components:

  1. Skill Development Training:The Udaan Scheme focuses on providing skill development training to the youth of Jammu and Kashmir in various sectors.
  2. Employment Opportunities: The scheme aims to create employment opportunities by connecting the skilled youth with potential employers and industries.
  3. Industry Participation:Udaan involves the active participation of industries and employers who collaborate to provide training and employment opportunities for the youth.
  4. Career Counseling:Career counseling and guidance are integral components of the scheme to help participants make informed decisions about their career paths.
  5. Financial Support: Financial support may be provided to cover the costs of training and other related expenses.
  6. Special Recruitment Drives: Special recruitment drives and job fairs are organized under the scheme to facilitate the placement of trained individuals.
