Sub-mission on Agricultural Mechanization

The SMAM scheme has both centrally sponsored and central sector scheme components. In the centrally sponsored scheme components, the Government of India funds 60% 
of the cost and the states’ share is 40% in all states except north-eastern and Himalayan states where the ratio is 90:10 wherein GOI funds 90%. In UTs, the central share is 100%.

Objectives of SMAM

  1. Conduct performance testing for various farm machineries and equipment at the four Farm Machinery Training and Testing Institutes (FMTTIs), designated State Agricultural Universities (SAUs) and ICAR institutions.
  2. Promote farm mechanization among stakeholders by way of on-field and off-field training and demonstrations.
  3. Provide financial assistance to farmers for procurement of farm machinery and implements.
  4. Establish custom hiring centres of the location and crop-specific farm machinery and implements.
  5. Provide financial assistance to small and marginal farmers for hiring machinery and implements in low-mechanized regions.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. All landholding farmers' families, Self Help Groups (SHGs), User Groups, Cooperative Societies, Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs) and Entrepreneurs.
  2. The farmer must be a native of India.
  3. Women farmers can also take advantage of this scheme. They will be given priority in this scheme.
  4. The benefit of this scheme will be given by the central government to those farmers who are financially weak.
  5. The benefit of this scheme will be given to those farmers who have not taken the benefit of subsidy from any other central scheme earlier.

Key Components:

  1. Custom Hiring Centers:Establish and promote custom hiring centers where farmers can access machinery on a rental basis, reducing the financial burden on individual farmers.
  2. Research and Development:Allocate funds for research and development in agricultural machinery to encourage innovation and the development of cost-effective technologies.
  3. Capacity Building:Conduct training programs for farmers, equipment operators, and extension workers to enhance their skills in operating and maintaining modern agricultural machinery.
  4. Financial Assistance: Clearly outline the financial assistance mechanisms provided to farmers, including subsidies, loans, grants, or any other financial incentives to promote the adoption of agricultural mechanization.
  5. Implementation Strategy:Describe the step-by-step plan for implementing the scheme, including the roles of various stakeholders such as government agencies, financial institutions, and extension services.
  6. Monitoring and Evaluation:Establish a robust monitoring and evaluation framework to assess the impact of the scheme, measure progress, and identify areas for improvement.
  7. Beneficiary Inclusion:Ensure that the scheme is inclusive, reaching out to small and marginal farmers, women farmers, and other vulnerable groups.
  8. Sustainability Measures:Outline measures to ensure the sustainability of the program, such as promoting eco-friendly technologies and efficient resource use.
