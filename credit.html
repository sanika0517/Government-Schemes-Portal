Kisan Credit Card Yojana

 The Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Yojana is a government-sponsored scheme in India that aims to provide farmers with timely and adequate credit to meet their agricultural and related 
needs. The scheme was launched to help farmers access formal credit institutions easily and at affordable rates.

Objectives of KCC

  1. The primary objective is to provide timely and adequate credit to farmers for their agricultural activities, including crop production, post-harvest expenses, working capital, and other farm-related expenses.

Eligibility Criteria:

  1. All farmers, including small and marginal farmers, tenant farmers, and sharecroppers, are eligible to apply for a Kisan Credit Card.
  2. The eligibility criteria may vary slightly among different banks, but the scheme is generally accessible to all farmers engaged in agriculture.

Key Components:

  1. Flexible Credit Limit: Farmers are provided with a revolving credit limit based on the scale of their operations and cropping pattern.
  2. Interest Subvention: The government often provides interest subvention to reduce the cost of credit for farmers. This means that farmers may get loans at a lower interest rate.
  3. Insurance Coverage: KCC holders may also be eligible for insurance coverage to protect them from unforeseen events such as crop failure, natural disasters, or accidental death.
